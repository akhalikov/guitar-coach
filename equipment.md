## Guitars

### Guitar 1 — Squier Affinity Telecaster (SH configuration)

- Type: electric, solid body
- Pickups: **single coil (neck) + humbucker (bridge)** — SH layout
- 5-way switch positions (typical Tele wiring):
  - **Position 1 (bridge)** — bridge humbucker. Bright, punchy, dense. Good for: Smoke on the Water, Miserlou (twangy surf with treble up), riffs needing humbucker thickness.
  - **Position 2** — bridge HB + neck SC (combined)
  - **Position 3** — neck single coil. Warm, round, forgiving. Good for: Knockin' on Heaven's Door, Stand By Me, Hurt, Ain't No Sunshine — anything clean and chord-based.
- Strings: (note gauge here — typical electric is 9–42 or 10–46)
- Last setup: —
- Notes: Tele is unforgiving with sloppy strumming — great for building clean technique.

### Guitar 2 — Squier Sonic Stratocaster (HSS configuration)

- Type: electric, solid body
- Pickups: **humbucker (bridge) + single coil (middle) + single coil (neck)** — HSS layout
- 5-way switch positions (Strat HSS wiring):
  - **Position 1 (bridge)** — bridge humbucker. Aggressive, thick, modern. Good for: Enter Sandman (heavy riffs + palm muting), Watching Over Me chorus, Zombie chorus.
  - **Position 2** — bridge HB + middle SC
  - **Position 3** — middle SC alone
  - **Position 4** — middle + neck SC (combined, "in-between" tone)
  - **Position 5 (neck)** — neck single coil. Warm, clean, vocal. Good for: Watching Over Me verse, Zombie verse, About Today, Hurt, ballads.

- Notes: Use **position 5** for clean verses, **position 1** for distorted choruses and riffs. Same guitar covers the dynamic range of most songs.

## Amp

- **Yamaha THR5** — desktop modeling amp
- Amp model channels used: **CLEAN** (most ballads), **CRUNCH** (Smoke on the Water, chorus distortion), **MODERN** (Enter Sandman main riff — best for palm muting and slides)

**Default tone for practice:**

- **Clean (chord work, ballads):** CLEAN, gain 1–2, master 4–5, tone 5, reverb low–medium (2–3).
- **Light crunch (rock riffs, choruses):** CRUNCH, gain 4–6, master 4–5, tone 5, reverb low or off.
- **Heavy modern (Metallica, metal riffs):** MODERN, gain 7–8, master 4–5, tone 4–5, reverb off.

Per-song specific settings are documented in each `songs/<song>.md`.

## Maintenance reminders

- **Re-string:** every 1–3 months depending on play time. Sweaty hands = sooner.
- **Wipe down strings after every session** with a dry cloth — extends string life noticeably.
- **Truss rod / setup:** if action feels high or frets buzz, take to a tech (don't DIY truss rod adjustments yet).
- **Humidity:** electrics are forgiving, but in very dry winter air a guitar humidifier is cheap insurance.

## Why two guitars

The Tele SH and Strat HSS cover different tonal worlds with minimal overlap:

- **Tele SH** — when you want **twang and definition** (clean chords, surf, country, rhythm work) or **a tight bridge humbucker** (classic rock riffs).
- **Strat HSS** — when you want **3-pickup versatility** (clean verses on neck) plus **modern high-gain** (bridge HB for metal and heavy rock).

For learning, the coach defaults to whichever guitar matches the song. If a song works on both, pick whichever needs the strings warmed up.
