## Why this song is special

- Written in 1993 as a political protest against the conflict in Northern Ireland
- Key of **E minor** — same as Hurt, Watching Over Me, Vykhoda Net
- Chords Em, C, G, D — all familiar by now
- A signature **repeating riff pattern** — hypnotic and meditative
- Dolores O'Riordan's voice — one of the most recognizable in 90s rock
- Ideal for imitating the vocal on guitar — short phrases with pauses

## What you're building

**Technique**
- A repeating pattern — trains steadiness and consistency
- Dynamics: quiet verse → powerful chorus
- Palm muting in the heavier sections
- Vocal phrasing — copying Dolores's inflections on the pentatonic

**Theory**
- E minor — already the fourth song in this key
- Em → C → G → D — the same classic progression
- Vibrato — a small finger wobble to imitate the "wail" of the voice

**Music history**
- The Cranberries — Irish band, 90s alternative rock
- Dolores O'Riordan died in 2018 — the song became even more tragic
- One example of rock music as political statement

## 🎸 Guitar and tone

### Which guitar to use?

**Squier Sonic Strat HSS** — whole song on one guitar, just change the selector position:

- **Verse** → position **5** (neck pickup) — warm, clean, SD-1 off
- **Chorus** → position **1** (bridge HB) — denser, SD-1 on

## 🎯 Focus when working on it

- Original tempo ~90 BPM — start at 70%
- The pattern repeats — get it automatic, then layer in the dynamics
- Listen to how Dolores phrases the vocal — try repeating those inflections on the pentatonic
- Vibrato: fret a note → small back-and-forth wobble with the finger

## 🎶 Improvising over this song

> 💡 **Which pentatonic fits?**
> The song is in **E minor**. Use **E minor pentatonic** — same position (open) as Hurt, Watching Over Me, and Vykhoda Net. Copy Dolores's vocal inflections — short phrases, pauses, light vibrato on long notes.

| String | Frets |
|---|---|
| E (6th) | 0 — 3 |
| A (5th) | 0 — 2 |
| D (4th) | 0 — 2 |
| G (3rd) | 0 — 2 |
| B (2nd) | 0 — 3 |
| E (1st) | 0 — 3 |

> 🎤 **Vocal phrasing:** Sing "In your head, in your head, zombie" — and find that melody on the fretboard. That's exactly how a crying guitar works.
