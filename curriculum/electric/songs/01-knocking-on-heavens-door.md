## Why this song is a perfect starting point

- Simple chords
- Slow tempo
- Forces you to work on **rhythm and tone**
- Sounds great on the Tele clean or with light overdrive

## What you're building

**Technique**
- Clean chord transitions
- Strumming control — light, soft attack
- Muting unplayed strings

**Theory**
- Chord progressions
- Feeling a major key
- Counting bars

**Tele focus**
- Neck pickup
- Even dynamics (the Tele is unforgiving of sloppy strumming)

## 🎸 Guitar and tone

### Which guitar to use?

**Squier Affinity Tele SH** — neck pickup (position 3, neckward side)

Knockin' is a clean, warm song. The Tele's neck pickup gives a soft, rounded tone — ideal for light strumming. The Strat's neck single-coil also works great — just as warm and just as forgiving.

### Yamaha THR5 — settings

| Parameter | Setting |
|---|---|
| Amp Type | CLEAN |
| Gain | 1–2 |
| Master | 4–5 |
| Bass | 5 |
| Mid | 5 |
| Treble | 5 |
| Reverb | Low–Medium (2–3) — adds warmth without washing out |

### BOSS SD-1

- Not needed for this song — keep it bypassed.
- The goal is a clean, transparent tone where your dynamics and attack do all the work.

## 🎶 Improvising over this song

> 💡 **Which pentatonic fits?**
> The song is in **G major**. Use **E minor pentatonic** — E minor is the relative minor of G major, so they share the same notes. Over time you can also try G major pentatonic — it sounds brighter, but to start, focus on one.

**Position 1 on the fretboard (E minor pentatonic):**

| String | Frets |
|---|---|
| E (6th) | 0 — 3 |
| A (5th) | 0 — 2 |
| D (4th) | 0 — 2 |
| G (3rd) | 0 — 2 |
| B (2nd) | 0 — 3 |
| E (1st) | 0 — 3 |

- Play single notes over the backing track — look for the ones that fit the mood
- Don't rush — pauses and simple phrases sound better than a stream of notes
