## Why this song is special

- Originally written by Trent Reznor (Nine Inch Nails, 1994), but Johnny Cash's 2002 version became legend
- Simple chords Am, C, G, D — all familiar by now
- **Arpeggio** — a new technique: chords played one string at a time instead of strummed
- Slow tempo — time to think about and feel every note
- Teaches you to play with emotion, not mechanically
- Em pentatonic fits for improv

## What you're building

**Technique**
- **Arpeggio (fingerpicking or pick)** — the main new skill
- Chord changes Am → C → G → D — familiar chords in a new context
- Dynamic control — soft and expressive

**Theory**
- Am — minor key, somber mood
- The progression Am → C → G → D — one of the most classic in rock and folk
- Em pentatonic for improv (Am = relative minor of C major)

**Music history**
- Johnny Cash recorded this at 71, a few months before his death
- The video was shot at his farm — one of the most emotional music videos in rock history
- An example of a cover that surpasses the original

## 🎯 Focus when working on it

**Arpeggio — a starting pattern:**

Instead of strumming, play the chord one string at a time top to bottom:

`5th string → 4th → 3rd → 2nd → 1st`

That's the classic fingerpicking pattern. You can do it with a pick too — one string at a time, down (↓), slowly and evenly.

- Start very slowly — original tempo is ~63 BPM
- Focus on evenness — every note the same volume
- Once the chord changes are automatic — add expression
- Pause and silence in this song matter more than the notes

## 🎶 Improvising over this song

> 💡 **Which pentatonic fits?**
> The song is in **A minor**. Use **E minor pentatonic** (the same position as Stand By Me and Knockin') — Am and Em belong to the same key group. Play over the backing track — it sounds beautiful.
