## Why this song is perfect

- Same chords as Knockin' on Heaven's Door (G, D, C) — but adds **Em**
- Em is your first practical taste of a minor chord by ear
- The rhythm is a touch faster than Knockin' — a good next step
- Great melody, doesn't get boring on repeats

## What you're building

**Technique**
- Automatic chord switching (same chords as Knockin', new rhythm)
- Slightly faster strumming — tempo control
- G → Em and Em → C transitions — new combinations

**Theory**
- **Em in context** — feel how a minor chord changes the mood
- Compare G major (bright) vs. Em (darker) in the same progression
- Chord progression: G → Em → C → D (a rock-and-roll classic)

**Music history**
- This progression (I–vi–IV–V) is one of the most-used in rock, pop, and soul
- B.B. King = direct heir to the blues tradition (Robert Johnson → Muddy Waters → B.B. King)

## 🎯 Focus when working on it

- Start at a slow tempo — drill the change to Em

- Strumming: steady, don't rush — original tempo is ~68 BPM
- Once the chords come automatically — sing or hum the melody along

## 🎶 Improvising over this song

> 💡 **Which pentatonic fits?**
> The song is in **G major**. Use **E minor pentatonic** — it shares the same notes as G major, because Em is the relative minor of G major.

**Position 1 on the fretboard (E minor pentatonic):**

| String | Frets |
|---|---|
| E (6th) | 0 — 3 |
| A (5th) | 0 — 2 |
| D (4th) | 0 — 2 |
| G (3rd) | 0 — 2 |
| B (2nd) | 0 — 3 |
| E (1st) | 0 — 3 |

- Start with a single note — for example, open E on the 6th string
- Play single notes over the backing track — that's already improvising
- Not all notes sound equal — find the ones that fit the mood
