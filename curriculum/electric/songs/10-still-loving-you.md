## Why this song is special

- One of the most emotional Scorpions ballads — written by Rudolf Schenker
- Key of **E minor** — same as Hurt, Zombie, Watching Over Me
- Chords Em, C, G, D — all familiar by now!
- Slow tempo — lots of room for expression
- E minor pentatonic — same position for improv
- Perfect prep for "Always Somewhere" (Stage 3)

## What you're building

**Technique**
- Arpeggio or soft strumming in the verse
- Dynamics: quiet in the verse, powerful in the chorus
- Attack control — this song is about feel, not speed

**Theory**
- E minor — fifth song in this key
- Same Em → C → G → D progression
- Vocal phrasing — Klaus Meine sings in very guitar-like phrases

**Music history**
- Still Loving You (1984) from the album *Love at First Sting*
- One of the great Scorpions hits — played a real role in the reunification of Germany
- Klaus Meine wrote it after losing his voice and thinking he'd never sing again

## 🎸 Guitar and tone

### Which guitar to use?

**Squier Sonic Strat HSS** — whole song on one guitar, just change the selector position:

- **Verse** → position **5** (neck pickup) — warm, soft tone, SD-1 off
- **Chorus** → position **1** (bridge HB) — denser, SD-1 on

## 🎯 Focus when working on it

- Original tempo ~65 BPM — very slow, every note matters
- Listen to how Klaus Meine sings — his phrases are literally guitar phrases
- Reverb in the verse creates atmosphere — don't take it off
- Pause between phrases works the same way as in Hurt

## 🎶 Improvising over this song

> 💡 **Which pentatonic fits?**
> The song is in **E minor**. Use **E minor pentatonic** — same position (open) as Hurt, Watching Over Me, and Zombie. Listen to Klaus Meine's vocal and copy his inflections on the fretboard.

| String | Frets |
|---|---|
| E (6th) | 0 — 3 |
| A (5th) | 0 — 2 |
| D (4th) | 0 — 2 |
| G (3rd) | 0 — 2 |
| B (2nd) | 0 — 3 |
| E (1st) | 0 — 3 |

> 🎤 **Vocal phrasing:** Klaus Meine sings very expressively with vibrato on long notes — try a light back-and-forth wobble with the finger on a fretted note.
