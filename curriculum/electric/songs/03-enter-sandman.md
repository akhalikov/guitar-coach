## Why this song is perfect

- One of the most famous riffs in rock music
- Teaches **palm muting** as a core technique
- Introduces **gallop rhythm** and **power chords** in a real musical context
- A great fit for the Strat HSS (bridge humbucker = dense, percussive tone)

## What you're building

**Technique**
- Palm muting (picking hand resting on the strings near the bridge)
- Power chords (root + fifth + octave)
- Alternate picking with muting control
- The gallop pattern (down-down-up)

**Theory**
- A minor key (E minor)
- Riff-based song structure
- Dynamics: muted vs. open notes

**Strat focus**
- Bridge humbucker (position 1) — dense, high-gain tone
- Precise, exact attack — the Strat is brutal about exposing sloppy muting

## 🎸 Guitar and tone

### Which guitar to use?

**Squier Sonic Strat HSS** — bridge humbucker (position 1)

This song wants a humbucker. The Tele SH also works, but the Strat's aggression suits Metallica better.

**Guitar tone knob:**
- Intro (clean): Tone 7
- Main riff (drive): Tone 8–10 — open up so the palm muting sounds tight

### Yamaha THR5 — settings

**Intro (SD-1 off):**

| Parameter | Setting |
|---|---|
| Amp Type | CLEAN |
| Gain | 2 |
| Master | 4–5 |
| Tone | 5 |
| Reverb | Off |

**Main riff — preferred path: THR5 MODERN (no SD-1, no intro):**

| Parameter | Setting |
|---|---|
| Amp Type | MODERN |
| Gain | 7–8 |
| Master | 4–5 |
| Tone | 4–5 |
| Reverb | Off |

> 💡 Best tone for palm muting and slides. Guitar tone knob: 8–10.

**Alternative: CRUNCH + SD-1 (Drive 6–7, Tone 5–6, Level 5–6):**

> Workable tone, but the slide on string 5 sounds less beautiful than THR5 MODERN. Fine for practicing palm muting; for a full song tone, MODERN is preferable.

## 🎯 Focus when working on it

- Start **very slowly** — focus on even palm muting
- Palm muting is good, but **don't mute everything** — this song is about drive and attack. At the right spots, lift the palm mute and let the note ring open
- Example: the open notes in the riff are the climax of the phrase, they should ring out aggressively
- Muted notes = a dull thump, open notes = ringing
- Metronome: start at 60 BPM, song tempo is ~123 BPM
- The riff is in Em — learn 2 bars at a time
