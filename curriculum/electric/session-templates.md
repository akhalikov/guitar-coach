# Session Templates

Three shapes — 15, 25, 45 minutes. The 25-minute template is the default; the others are for short days and longer Saturday sessions.

Every template has the same five elements in the same order: **tune → warm-up → technique → song → theory**. The split changes based on time. Theory always comes last so it lands while the hands are still warm and remembers what they just did.

## 15-minute session (short day)

| Block | Time | What |
|---|---|---|
| Tune & posture check | 1 min | Tune up. Check sitting position, fretting hand thumb on back of neck, picking hand relaxed. |
| Warm-up | 2 min | Chromatic finger exercise (1-2-3-4 up each string, frets 5-6-7-8) at 60 BPM. |
| Technique focus | 6 min | Current JustinGuitar lesson — the one drill that matters most today. Pick 1, do it deep. |
| Song / application | 4 min | One song or one section of a song using today's technique. |
| Theory snippet | 2 min | Tied to today's lesson. One sentence, one fretboard example, repeat back. |

## 25-minute session (default — daily)

| Block | Time | What |
|---|---|---|
| Tune & posture check | 1 min | Tune up. Posture check. |
| Warm-up | 3 min | Chromatic 1-2-3-4 up the neck, both ascending and descending. Optional: spider exercise. |
| Technique focus | 10 min | Current JustinGuitar module's main drill. 2–3 rounds with check-ins between. |
| Song / application | 7 min | One song using today's chords/technique. Play through, fix the rough spot, play through again. |
| Theory snippet | 3 min | One concept from `theory-book.md`, applied on the fretboard. Artur repeats it back. |
| Wrap & log | 1 min | One-line takeaway. Claude writes the log. |

## 45-minute session (weekend / deeper day)

| Block | Time | What |
|---|---|---|
| Tune & posture check | 1 min | Tune up. Posture check. |
| Warm-up | 5 min | Chromatic exercises + one finger-independence drill (e.g. spider 1-3-2-4) at 70 BPM. |
| Technique focus A | 10 min | Current JustinGuitar lesson — main drill, several rounds. |
| Technique focus B | 8 min | A second drill from the same module OR a callback to a struggling spot from last week. |
| Song work | 12 min | One song deep: break into sections, slow it down, then play to a backing track. Or 2 songs in rotation. |
| Theory deep dive | 6 min | One concept, but with multiple fretboard examples and an exercise (e.g. find the same note in 3 places on different strings). |
| Repertoire pass | 2 min | Quick play-through of one already-known song to keep it in shape. |
| Wrap & log | 1 min | Takeaway, struggle to revisit, log entry. |

## Block-by-block guidance for the coach

### Tune & posture check
Always. A guitar drifts out of tune between sessions. 30 seconds with a tuner saves 25 minutes of practicing with bad reference pitch.

Posture cues: sit tall, guitar body resting on right thigh (right-handed) or left (left-handed), neck angled slightly up (10–15°), fretting-hand thumb on the *back* of the neck (not over the top — that's an acoustic strumming-only habit). Pick gripped between thumb pad and side of index, the rest of the hand relaxed.

### Warm-up
Goal: get blood into the fingers, not to learn anything new. Default drill is the chromatic 1-2-3-4: index on fret 5, middle on 6, ring on 7, pinky on 8 — play those four notes, then the same on string 5, string 4, etc. all the way to string 1. Then reverse.

Add tempo only when the notes are clean. 60 BPM, quarter notes, with a metronome. Don't race the warm-up.

### Technique focus
This is the meat of the session — current JustinGuitar lesson. Do **one specific drill** rather than 3 superficial passes. A drill looks like:

> Change between A and D for 1 minute. Count clean transitions. Report the count.

Or:

> Play E major chord, strum 4 down-strums, mute, strum 4 again. Repeat 8 times.

Concrete > vague. Always pick a measurable thing.

### Song / application
The reward block. Use chords/technique just practiced in an actual song. Pick from `songs.md`. Don't introduce a new song every day — rotate the current 1–3.

If a song has a rough spot, isolate the 2 bars that don't work, loop them at half-tempo, then put them back into the song.

### Theory snippet
3–5 minutes max. One concept. Use the fretboard. Format:

1. **State the concept** in one sentence.
2. **Show it** on the guitar (or with a TAB block in chat).
3. **Ask Artur to repeat it** in his own words or play the example.
4. **Note it** in the log so it can be reinforced over the next few sessions.

If Artur didn't get it, re-explain once differently — don't grind a third time. The concept will come back around.

### Wrap & log
Single-line takeaway from Artur ("F chord muting still bad, A→D much better"). Coach writes the log to `logs/YYYY/MM-DD.md` using the template in `logs/TEMPLATE.md`, then updates `progress.md`.

## Tempo floor for new things

When learning a new chord, riff, or drill: start at **half** the eventual target tempo. Only speed up when the slow version is clean for 3 consecutive passes. The metric is cleanliness, not BPM.

Common target BPMs for Grade 1–3 drills:
- Chord changes: 60 BPM (Grade 1) → 80 (Grade 2) → 100 (Grade 3)
- Single-note runs / scales: 60 → 80 → 100+
- Strumming patterns: stay at song tempo, ideally with a metronome or backing track
