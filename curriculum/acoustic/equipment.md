## Tone — no amp, no pedal

Unlike electric, there's no signal chain to configure. Tone comes from touch: pick vs. fingers, attack strength, and where the strike happens relative to the soundhole (closer to the neck = warmer/rounder, closer to the bridge = brighter/more percussive). Coach these variables directly instead of amp/pedal settings.

## Maintenance reminders

- **Humidity matters more than for the electrics.** Solid spruce top — a guitar humidifier is worth having, especially in dry winter air. Watch for fret-edge sharpness or top sinking as humidity warning signs.
- **Re-string:** every 1–3 months depending on play time, same cadence as electric. Coated strings (EXP11) resist corrosion longer than uncoated.
- **Wipe down strings after every session** with a dry cloth.
- **Truss rod / setup:** if action feels high or frets buzz, take to a tech — don't DIY.

## Why acoustic, alongside the two electrics

Different technique context: no pickups/amp means tone lives entirely in the hands, string tension is higher (real strength/callus benefit), and the wider neck changes hand geometry slightly. The JustinGuitar/Stine curriculum content doesn't change — this guitar is a different surface to apply the same sequence on, plus its own space for fingerstyle exploration.
