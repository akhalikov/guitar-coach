## Why this song is here

- All chords (Em, C, Am, D, G) are already in the student's vocabulary from JustinGuitar Grade 1 — this song is a rhythm challenge, not a chord-vocabulary challenge
- Good pairing with Это всё: same songwriter, overlapping chord families (Em/C/D/G recur), but a different technical focus — strumming precision instead of fingerstyle
- Recognizable, motivating repertoire target

## Structure & chords

**Intro:** Em7 ×4, then (Em C | Am D/F# | Em C | Am D) ×2
**Verse:** Em D Em C Am D — repeated per line, two lines per verse
**Chorus:** D G D Em C | G D G C | D G D/F# Em C | G D Em

## What you're building

**Technique**
- **Strum pattern** — the chords are simple but the rhythm isn't trivial. Start with a plain down-strum on every beat to lock in the chord changes, then layer in a pattern like ↓ ↓↑↓↑↓ once the changes are automatic
- D/F# reappears here (same slash chord as in Это всё and Dust in the Wind) — by this point it should be a known shape, not a new one
- Em7 as an intro-only color — one-finger lift off the standard Em shape

**Theory**
- E minor as home key — same key family as several electric songs already in rotation (Watching Over Me, Vykhoda Net, Zombie), so the fretboard familiarity should transfer
- Chord changes stay diatonic to E minor throughout (i–VII–VI–etc. territory) — good real-world example when Stine gets to diatonic chords in a key

## 🎯 Focus when working on it

- Nail the chord-change timing first with the simplest possible strum (all down-strums) before adding pattern complexity
- Isolate the D/F# transitions — moving the bass note under a D shape while everything else stays put is the one genuinely fiddly part
- Once the verse strum is solid, layer in the chorus's slightly busier pattern (D G D Em C) as its own drill before running the full song
