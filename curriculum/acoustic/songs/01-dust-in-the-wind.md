## Why this song is here

- The defining Travis-picking standard — thumb keeps a steady alternating bass while index/middle add the melody on top, the core skill this song is chosen to build
- All open chords, no barre — approachable once the picking-hand independence is there
- A real fingerstyle showcase for the Baton Rouge's wide neck (more room between strings makes the thumb/finger separation easier to feel)
- Well-known enough that a clean run-through is immediately recognizable — good for motivation

## What you're building

**Technique**
- **Travis picking** — the main new skill. Thumb alternates between the root and 5th (or another bass note) in a steady eighth-note pulse; index and middle fingers pick out the syncopated top notes in between
- Chord vocabulary: Cmaj7, Cadd9, Asus2, Asus4 — first exposure to "add" and "sus" chord colors, useful vocabulary for the Stine theory track too
- Slash chords (G/B, D/F#) — bass note different from the root, a preview of voice-leading

**Theory**
- C major / A minor relative pair — same key signature (no sharps/flats), reinforces the relative-minor concept from Stine's Module 3
- Sus2/sus4 as "chords with the 3rd swapped out" — ties into the Cool Chords module later in JustinGuitar Grade 2

## 🎯 Focus when working on it

**Getting the Travis pattern going:**
- Start thumb-only: alternate bass notes on the beat, no melody yet, until it's automatic and doesn't need watching
- Add index/middle on top once the thumb is steady — resist the urge to speed up before the hands are independent
- Practice at well under 96 BPM (60–70 is reasonable) — this song is fast at tempo and not worth chasing speed before the pattern is clean

**Chord-to-chord smoothness:**
- Asus2 → Asus4 → Am is a common shape-shift in the intro/chorus — practice it in isolation as its own drill
- G/B and D/F# just move the bass note under an otherwise familiar shape — isolate the bass-note change before worrying about the full picking pattern over it
