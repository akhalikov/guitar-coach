## Why this song is here

- Closing song of every ДДТ concert — culturally significant to the student, strong motivation
- No barre chords — G, D/F#, Em, C, D, Cadd9 are all open-position shapes already familiar or close to it from the JustinGuitar sequence
- Mixes two techniques in one song: перебор (fingerstyle picking pattern) for the verses, бой (rhythmic strumming) for the third verse and choruses — a natural bridge exercise between the fingerstyle work (Hurt, Dust in the Wind) and the strumming repertoire (Knockin', Stand By Me)

## Structure & chords

**Verses 1 & 2 (перебор):** G → D/F# → Em → C D
**Chorus:** G → D/F# → Em → C | G → D → Cadd9 → D
**Verse 3 (бой):** same G → D/F# → Em → C D progression, switched to strumming
**Outro:** G → D/F# → Em → C → D, repeated 8x

## What you're building

**Technique**
- **Перебор pattern** — one commonly used version: bass note, then 3-2-1 (or 3-2-3-1 depending on preference) picked in sequence. Start with a simple bass–3–2–1 and let it settle before trying variations.
- **Бой (strum) pattern** for verse 3 and chorus: ↓↑↓ ↓ ↓↑ — practice slowly with a metronome before matching it to the chord changes
- D/F# — bass note on the 4th string 2nd fret under a D shape; same slash-chord concept as Dust in the Wind's G/B

**Theory**
- G major — no relative-minor detour needed here, straightforward diatonic progression (I–V/iii–vi–IV–V, roughly)
- Cadd9 — same "add" chord color introduced in Dust in the Wind; reinforcing that vocabulary across two songs is deliberate

## 🎯 Focus when working on it

- Get the chord changes solid with basic down-strums first — don't try to layer перебор or the exact bой pattern onto shaky chord changes
- Once changes are clean, add the перебор pattern to verses 1–2 only, keep verse 3 and the chorus as plain strums until both feel natural on their own
- Combine the two techniques in one pass only once each is independently solid — this is the actual milestone for this song, not just playing the chords
